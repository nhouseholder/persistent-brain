## Why we did it

The old Kahneman v2.0 framework assumed models needed SLOW mode by default. For 2026 models with strong native reasoning, this wastes tokens and creates artificial deliberation. v3.0 inverts the default: FAST first, escalate only with justification. This matches actual model capabilities and reduces token burn.

## How we did it

- `reasoning_tracker.py` — new module, zero dependencies, pure stdlib. Persists state to `~/.unified-brain/reasoning_state.json`.
- `brain_router.py` — extended `handle_request()` to auto-count research tool calls as evidence pulls. Added 3 new tool handlers.
- Budgets: FAST=0 pulls/200 tokens, DELIBERATE=1 pull/1500 tokens, SLOW=3 pulls/unlimited.
- Completion Gate Lite — 4-question internal check for SLOW mode (conclusion, evidence for, evidence against, what would change your mind).
